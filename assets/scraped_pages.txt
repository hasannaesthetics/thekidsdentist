                       <div style="display: inline-flex;"><span class="sectiontxt"><b><a name="our-practice">Our Practice :</a></b></span></div>                       

                       <hr/>

                       <p>The Kids Dentist is a dental practice providing specialised dentistry for infants, children and adolescents in Singapore. The Kids Dentist recommends that every child visits the dentist by his/her 1st birthday, as advocated by the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry. This is to avoid potential oral health care problems in your child.</p>

                       <p>Parents do play a role in making the first visit for their child to the dentist enjoyable and positive. Your child should be informed of the visit and told that the dentist will count and clean his/her teeth. The dentist will also show him/her how to care for their teeth. It is best not to use words such as needle, pain, drill or hurt around your child as it might cause unnecessary fear. The Kids Dentist makes it a practice to use words that convey the same message, but are pleasant and non-frightening to the child.</p>

                       <p>Your child's first visit will include a complete examination performed by our paediatric dentists. Radiographs (x-rays) may be obtained of your child's teeth if necessary. You are encouraged to accompany your child into the examination room. Following the clinical examination, our dentists will advise you on the appropriate treatment required for your child and answer any questions you may have.</p>

                       <p>While visiting our clinic, your child is welcome to enjoy the toys, books, TV games as well as watch DVD movies while sitting on the dental chair.</p>

                        <span class="sectiontxt"><b>BEHAVIOUR MODIFICATION & PAIN MANAGEMENT:</b></span><br/><br/>

                        <div class="accordion">  

                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-1">Behaviour modification and shaping<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            <div id="accordion-1" class="accordion-section-content">                                                                                 

                                              Our team of dentists are specially trained and equipped to help anxious children feel safe and comfortable during dental treatment. Our clinic is designed for children and our staff are patient, caring and most importantly, love being with kids.

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->                                            

                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-2">Computerised local anaesthesia delivery system<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            <div id="accordion-2" class="accordion-section-content">  

                                                                                   

                                            The Kids Dentist is constantly implementing new technology to help us deliver dental care in the most comfortable manner for children. For treatment requiring numbing of the tooth, we use a computerised sensor-controlled local anaesthetic system instead of a traditional needle syringe. This helps to reduce the discomfort associated with injections and minimise the anxiety for the child.

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->

                                            

                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-3">Oral sedation and laughing gas sedation<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>                                           

                                            <div id="accordion-3" class="accordion-section-content">

                                           

                                            Our practice uses either oral sedation or nitrous oxide (aka "laughing gas") sedation to help the anxious child relax and allow the dentist to safely perform the necessary dental treatment. Our practice does not advocate the use of intravenous sedation for the management of children.                                           



                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->

                                            

                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-4">General anaesthesia<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            

                                            <div id="accordion-4" class="accordion-section-content">                                             

                                            

                                            In cases where the child is extremely anxious, comprehensive dental treatment may be performed under general anaesthesia (GA). Our practice engages only well-qualified paediatric anaesthetists for GA.                                             



                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->      

                                          

                                      </div>

                                      <div>&nbsp;</div>

                                        <span class="sectiontxt"><b>RESTORATIVE DENTISTRY:</b></span><br/><br/>

                                        <div class="accordion">     



                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-8">Paediatric crowns (aesthetic and stainless steel)<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            <div id="accordion-8" class="accordion-section-content">  

                                                                                     

                                             A stainless steel or a white zirconia aesthetic crown is usually recommended when there is extensive damage to multiple surfaces of the baby tooth from cavities or trauma. Paediatric crowns generally last longer than traditional fillings. Stainless steel and white zirconia crowns have their respective advantages and disadvantages. Feel free to consult with a <a href="http://www.thekidsdentist.com.sg/">child dental specialist</a> on what would be best suited for your child.

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->   



                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-7">Root canal treatment (pulpotomy and pulpectomy) for baby teeth<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            <div id="accordion-7" class="accordion-section-content"> 

                                                                                      

                                              A cavity (dental decay) that involves the nerve (pulp) of the tooth may cause a child to have a bad toothache. Such badly affected tooth will require a root canal treatment in order to restore it back to health. Depending on the extent of bacterial invasion in the root canal space of the tooth, a pulpotomy or pulpectomy may be indicated. A pulpotomy consists of removal of the top half portion of the nerve in the tooth while pulpectomy involves the removal of all the nerve in the root canal system of the tooth. Medicaments are used to disinfect the root canal as well. Following a root canal treatment, a crown is recommended to provide reinforcement for the tooth.



                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->     



                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-9">Leongâs premolars (aka dens evaginatus)<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            <div id="accordion-9" class="accordion-section-content">  

                                                                                    

                                            Some adult premolar teeth may have a protruded portion of tooth structure on the biting surface, which may fracture easily and expose the nerve (pulp) of the tooth. Preventive restoration can be placed if the protrusion is identified early. For young premolar teeth with infection of the pulp, a technique called revascularisation (or regenerative endodontics) can be done to treat such teeth. 

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->



                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-10">White fillings without mercury<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            <div id="accordion-10" class="accordion-section-content">

                                                                                       

                                            For the safety of our patients and the environment, we do not use mercury-containing silver (amalgam) fillings in our practice.

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->                                          

                                      </div>

                                        <div>&nbsp;</div>

                                        <span class="sectiontxt"><b>DENTAL TRAUMA AND SPORTS DENTISTRY:</b></span><br/><br/>

                                        <div class="accordion">  

                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-5">Management of fractured teeth<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            <div id="accordion-5" class="accordion-section-content">    

                                                                                 

                                              We provide immediate management for fractured and displaced primary and permanent teeth. Should your child suffer from a dental trauma, we advise that you make a dental appointment for your child promptly so that appropriate treatment and/or advice can be rendered. We also manage long-term trauma complications such as abscess and discolouration.



                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->

                                            

                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-6">Sports mouthguard<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            

                                            <div id="accordion-6" class="accordion-section-content">

                                          

                                            We recommend sports mouthguard for children and youths who are involved in contact sports to prevent traumatic injury to the teeth. In our practice, we fabricate customised sports mouthguard.

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->

                                          

                                      </div>                                      

                                      <div>&nbsp;</div>

                                        <span class="sectiontxt"><b>PREVENTIVE & MINIMAL INTERVENTION DENTISTRY:</b></span><br/><br/><br/>

                                        <div class="accordion">  

                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-11">Silver diamine fluoride<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            <div id="accordion-11" class="accordion-section-content">           

                                                                          

                                              Silver diamine fluoride is a topical, non-invasive medicament that can be applied to the dental cavity to help arrest the progression of dental decay. It can be an effective way to stabilise dental decay but also has its drawbacks. Discuss with our paediatric dentists to see if your child is a suitable candidate for silver diamine fluoride application.



                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->

                                            

                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-12">Home and professional fluoride therapies<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>

                                            <div id="accordion-12" class="accordion-section-content">

                                                                                     

                                            Fluoride has been proven beneficial in the prevention of tooth decay and tooth sensitivity. Fluoride treatment and application will strengthen your childâs teeth against tooth decay. We customise our fluoride therapy according to the dental decay risk of the child. 

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->



                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-13">Pit and fissure sealants<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>                                            

                                            <div id="accordion-13" class="accordion-section-content">

                                                                                    

                                            The chewing surfaces of your child's teeth are not smooth and flat. They are criss-crossed with tiny hills and valleys, called pits and fissures. These are places where plaque can build up and cause cavities. Sealants will prevent tooth decay especially in newly erupted permanent molars.

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->





                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-14">Removable orthodontics<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>                                            

                                            <div id="accordion-14" class="accordion-section-content">

                                                                                    

                                            Our practice can help to fabricate simple retainer-type braces to correct a simple crossbite, usually the anterior.

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->



                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-15">Space maintainers<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>                                            

                                            <div id="accordion-15" class="accordion-section-content">

                                                                                   

                                            A space maintainer (or spacer) is recommended to preserve a space in the arch if a tooth is lost prematurely. Space maintainers can help to prevent shifting and crowding in the adult dentition.

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->



                                            <div class="accordion-section">

                                            <a class="accordion-section-title accordionButton" href="#accordion-16">Habit-breaking appliance<span class="plusMinus">+</span></a>                                            

                                            <div id="accordion-16" class="accordion-section-content">

                                                                            

                                            If you child has an oral habit such as sucking thumb or the pacifier, the growth of the jaws and the teeth may be affected overtime, resulting in aesthetic and speech problems. At The Kids Dentist, we offer advice and strategies as well as fabricate custom-made oral appliances to help your child to stop these habits. 

                                            

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-content-->

                                            </div><!--end .accordion-section-->

                        <ol class="itemslist">
                            <li><b>WHAT IS A PAEDIATRIC DENTIST?</b><br /><a href="http://www.thekidsdentist.com.sg/home.html">Paediatric dentists</a> are dentists who only treat children. They are the paediatricians of dentistry. A paediatric dentist has extra and specialised training and is dedicated to the oral health of children. Children of all ages (including those with special health needs) need different approaches in dealing with their behaviour at the dentist. Paediatric dentists also guide childrenâs dental growth and development as well as help these children avoid future dental problems.</li>

                            <li><b>WHAT AGE GROUP OF CHILDREN DO YOU TREAT AT THE KIDS DENTIST?</b><br />We treat children from birth to 18 years.</li>

                            <li><b>HOW DO I FIND YOUR CLINIC?</b><br />A map, shuttle bus link and general directions to The Kids Dentist can be found in our âContact Usâ section.</li>

                            <li><b>CAN I LEARN MORE ABOUT THE STAFF?</b><br />The Kids Dentist staff information and photographs are part of this site.</li>

                            <li><b>WHAT ARE THE CHARGES LIKE AT THE KIDS DENTIST?</b><br />Our consultation charges range from $90-$110. Scaling and polishing charges are from $75 onwards while fillings are from $120-$160. We use only tooth-coloured fillings that do not contain mercury and safe for your child.</li>

                            <li><b>WHEN SHOULD MY CHILD FIRST SEE A DENTIST?</b><br />The American Academies of Pediatric Dentistry and Pediatrics recommend that children see a paediatric dentist by the childâs 1st birthday to prevent tooth decay and gum disease in the future.</li>

                            <li><b>WHY IS PRIMARY OR âBABYâ TEETH SO IMPORTANT?</b><br />Primary teeth, or baby-teeth, are important for (1) proper chewing and eating, (2) providing space for the permanent teeth and guiding them into the correct position, as well as (3) permitting normal development of the jaw bones and muscles. Primary teeth also affect the (4) development of speech and add to an (5) attractive appearance. While the front 4 teeth last until 6-7 years of age, the back teeth (ie molars) arenât replaced until ages 10-13.</li>

                            <li><b>WHAT DENTAL PROBLEMS COULD A BABY HAVE?</b><br />A big concern is nursing or baby bottle tooth decay. A baby risks severe tooth decay when he or she nurses continuously from a bottle of milk formula, breastmilk or juice during naps or at night. However, the earlier the dental visit, the better the chance of parents and caregivers preventing dental problems.</li>

                            <li><b>WHAT TO DO IN AN EMERGENCY?</b><br />Knowing what to do in a dental emergency is very important. Find helpful tips by reading the questions below on exactly what to do if an emergency happens in your family.</li>

                            <li><b>WHAT SHOULD I DO IF MY CHILD HAS A TOOTHACHE?</b><br />The parent can begin by cleaning around the painful tooth thoroughly. Use warm salt water and rinse the mouth to displace any food trapped between teeth. DO NOT use aspirin or paracetamol on the aching tooth or on the gum. If the child has a facial swelling, apply a cold compress to the area. For temporary pain relief, paracetamol (e.g. syrup Panadol or Calpol) is recommended. See a dentist as soon as possible.</li>

                            <li><b>WHAT SHOULD I DO IF MY CHILD HAS A BROKEN TOOTH?</b><br />The parent should rinse the area with warm water. Put a cold compress over the facial area of the injury. Recover any broken tooth fragments and get immediate dental attention.</li>

                            <li><b>WHAT IF MY CHILD HAS A KNOCKED-OUT PERMANENT TOOTH?</b><br />The parent has to recover the tooth and be sure to hold it by the crown (top white portion) and not the root end. Rinse, but do not clean or handle the tooth more than necessary. Reinsert the tooth in the socket and hold it in place using a clean piece of gauze or cloth. If the tooth cannot be reinserted, carry it in a cup containing fresh milk or saliva. If the patient is old enough, the tooth may also be carried in the patientâs mouth. The patient must see a dentist IMMEDIATELY! Time is a critical factor in saving the tooth.</li>

                            <li><b>WHAT SHOULD I DO IF MY CHILD HAS A CUT OR BITTEN TONGUE, LIP OR CHEEK?</b><br />Ice can be applied to any bruised areas. For bleeding, parents should apply firm (but gentle) pressure with a clean cloth. If the bleeding does not stop with pressure or continues after 15 minutes, go to an emergency centre.</li>
