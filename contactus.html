General Questions

A paediatric dentist is a dental specialist who has completed additional postgraduate training — beyond the standard dental degree — specifically in the oral health care of infants, children and adolescents, including those with special health care needs.

We treat children from birth to 18 years of age. We also see children with special health care needs regardless of age.

The Kids Dentist recommends that every child visits the dentist by his/her 1st birthday, as advocated by the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry. This avoids potential oral health care problems and helps establish a dental home early.

Our charges are based on the specific treatments required. As a guide: Consultation S$90–110; Scaling & polishing from S$75; White fillings S$120–160. All fillings are mercury-free. We are a Baby Bonus approved institution — CDA funds may be used.

Primary teeth are important for chewing, guiding the position of permanent teeth, jaw and facial muscle development, speech, and appearance. The back baby teeth are only replaced between the ages of 10–13, so they need to last a long time.

One of the most common is "Baby Bottle Tooth Decay" — cavities caused by prolonged exposure to sugary liquids (milk, formula, juice). Never put a baby to sleep with a bottle, as this can cause rapid decay of the front teeth.

Yes. The Kids Dentist is a Baby Bonus Approved Institution registered with the Ministry of Social and Family Development. You may use your Child Development Account (CDA) funds to offset the cost of your child's dental treatment.

Dental Emergencies

Rinse the mouth with warm salt water. Place a cold compress on the face if there is swelling. Give paracetamol for pain — do not place aspirin or paracetamol directly on the tooth. Call us and come in as soon as possible.

Rinse the mouth with warm water. Place a cold compress on the face to reduce swelling. Save any broken fragments. Seek immediate dental attention — the sooner you come in, the better the outcome for the tooth.

Act immediately. Hold the tooth by the crown (white part), not the root. Rinse gently — do not scrub. Try to reinsert it in the socket. If not possible, store it in milk or between the cheek and gum. Come in IMMEDIATELY — replanting within the hour gives the best chance of saving the tooth.

Apply ice to bruised areas. Apply firm but gentle pressure with a clean cloth. If bleeding does not stop after 15 minutes, or cannot be controlled, take your child to an emergency centre immediately.
